No-Sugar-Added Apple Pie

This recipe gets the apple's natural sweetness from antioxidant and fiber-rich dates. The cider vinegar added helps to enhance the flavor.

Active Time: 15 mins

Total Time: 3 hrs 10 mins

Servings: 10 servings

A day an apple keeps the doctors away

Ingredients:


  • 2 (9-inch) frozen pie shells, preferably whole-wheat
  • 5 pitted dates
  • 2 to 4 tablespoons water, divided
  • 5 medium Gala apples, unpeeled, cored and sliced 1/4-inch thick (7 cups)
  • 3 teaspoons cider vinegar
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon

Directions:

  1. -Preheat oven to 375°F. Remove pie shells from freezer to thaw slightly at room temperature.
  2. -Combine dates and 2 tablespoons water in a food processor; process until a thick, mostly smooth paste forms, 1 to 2 minutes. Add more water if needed to reach desired consistency.
  3. -Transfer the date paste to a large bowl; add apples, vinegar, and cinnamon. Stir until the apples are coated.
  4. -Transfer apple mixture to one pie shell and spread evenly. Flip the second crust on top, seal edges with a fork, and cut slits in the top crust.
  5. -Bake on a rimmed baking sheet for 45–55 minutes until golden brown and tender. Cool on a wire rack for about 2 hours.
Nutrition Profile:

Sesame-Free, Nut-Free, Dairy-Free, Soy-Free, Vegan, Vegetarian

Why we should consume less sugar?

Eating low sugar is important for maintaining overall health and preventing several health issues. Consuming less sugar helps regulate blood sugar levels, provides sustained energy, and reduces cravings for unhealthy foods. High sugar intake can lead to weight gain, increased risk of diabetes, heart disease, and tooth decay.
